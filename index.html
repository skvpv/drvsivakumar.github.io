About

Building intelligent systems for challenging environments.

Dr. V. Sivakumar is an Associate Professor in the School of Computer Engineering at the Manipal Institute of Technology (MIT), Manipal Academy of Higher Education (MAHE), and a Senior Member of the IEEE. He holds a B.E. in Computer Science & Engineering, an M.E. in Information Technology (Software Engineering), and a Ph.D. in Computer Science Engineering from VIT, Chennai, where his doctoral work addressed underwater acoustic sensor node scheduling and energy-efficient routing using evolutionary algorithms.

His research sits at the meeting point of underwater acoustic sensor networks, evolutionary and nature-inspired optimization, and machine learning. A central thread is the design of energy-efficient, reliable data-collection strategies for three-dimensional underwater wireless sensor networks — drawing on genetic and memetic algorithms, sink mobility, and, more recently, reinforcement learning. In parallel, he applies machine learning and deep learning to clinical decision support, network security, agriculture, and environmental analytics.

Across more than a decade in academia — at VIT, SRM IST, Vel Tech, and now MIT Manipal — his work appears in venues such as IEEE Access, the Journal of Big Data, Computer Communications, and Intelligence-Based Medicine. He holds five granted patents and design registrations, supervises doctoral scholars, and serves on doctoral advisory committees and journal review boards. He welcomes collaboration with researchers, students, and industry partners working on intelligent networked systems and applied AI.

Research

Intelligent systems for the ocean, the clinic, and the network edge.

A research program that pairs evolutionary and learning-based methods with hard, real-world systems — from three-dimensional underwater sensor networks to medical imaging and network security.

Core focus

Underwater Acoustic Sensor Networks

Energy-efficient node scheduling, lifetime enhancement, localization, and 3D mobility control for underwater wireless sensor networks.

Methods

Evolutionary & Nature-Inspired Optimization

Genetic, memetic, flower-pollination, Archimedes, and snake-swarm algorithms applied to NP-hard networking and resource-allocation problems.

Methods

Machine Learning & Deep Learning

Classification, prediction, and ensemble models spanning health, security, education, and environmental domains.

Applications

Computer Vision & Medical Imaging

Decision-support systems built on dental orthopantomograms, lung imaging, and other diagnostic image analysis.

Applications

IoT, Edge & Network Security

Energy scheduling, edge resource allocation, and machine-learning-driven detection of advanced persistent threats.

Applications

Applied AI, LLMs & Environmental Analytics

Large language models for agricultural knowledge transfer, and time-series analysis of long-term climate patterns.
